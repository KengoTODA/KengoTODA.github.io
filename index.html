History as a developer

I love software development.

When I was 15 years old (at 2001), My parents bought a PC (Windows ME) for me. I had motivation to make own PC game, but I didn't know how. I searched about it, and found that what I need is programming. This is why I learned about programming. I enjoyed game programming, 3D programming, text manipulation, SQLite, Win32 API, graphic programming and released some free-software.

At the same time, I also need to make a web page for my biology club. That site became very good chance to learn about HTML, CSS and JavaScript. I didn't care about responsibility so that site has a lot of onclick attribute and style attribute. I found why responsibility is important when I built big game like STG and Tetris.

From 2008, I have worked as a professional R&D at an ERP package vendor in Japan. I have learned about JVM, RDBMS, NoSQL, security and development process improvement.

And now I am also responsible for building new office in foreign countries. I am working 'globally': from 2011 to 2013 at Shanghai (as senior engineer), from 2013 at Singapore (as manager). What I do is not only guiding new member to understand culture and professionalism, but also helping old member in Japan to understand new member, new office and communicate with them.

JVM

I understand basis of JVMS, coredump & heapdump analyzation, and JVM bytecode.

With help from VisualVM or JProfiler, I can profile application (mainly web application) to understand what is bottleneck and/or why it costs much Java heap. Performance improvement needs knowledge and experience on a wide variety including JVM architecture, algorithm, memory model, hardware, middleware and library. It is not easy task. My profiling can help my client's business and my team's problem solving.

Reading JVM bytecode is good way to understand running system. I use javap to read class file.
